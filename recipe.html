MY FAVORITE NIGERIAN SOUP RECIPE(BANGA SOUP)

About Banga Soup

Soup and stews are a significant component of African cuisine and we have a whole lot of them. Banga soup also called Palm nut soup, is a soup prepared from palm fruit and it originated from the Niger Delta region of Nigeria. It is a special delicacy, particularly among the Urhobo and Itsekiri people of Delta State, and has become popular throughout Nigeria and West Africa.

Banga soup is not only popular in Nigeria, but it's also a delicacy in Ghana and Cameroon. In Ghana, palm nut soup is called Abenkwan. In Cameroon, it's known as Mbanga. While in Nigeria, it is called Banga soup, with variant names such as ofe akwu, oghwo amiedi, or izuwo ibiedi depending on the tribe, the ingredients used, and the mode of preparation.

Today, we will be preparing banga soup the way it is done by the Urhobo people of Delta state. Banga soup is traditionally eaten with "swallow" like starch, fufu, eba or pounded yam.

Recipe Information:

  • Prep time: 1hr
  • Cook time: 40mins
  • Servings: 3 Plates
  • Difficulty: Beginner

Ingredients

  • Palm nuts
  • 2 Fresh cat fish
  • 1.5 Ib Beef
  • Sea food (shrimp, crayfish, stock fish and periwinkles)
  • 2 Habanero or scotch bonnet pepper
  • 1 large onion
  • 2 teaspoon seasoning powder or Bouillon cubes
  • Salt
  • 1 teaspoon Cameroon or cayanne pepper
  • 1 Oburunbebe stick (Licorice root)
  • 1 tablespoon of Banga spices (Aidan fruit, Ataiko, Irugege seeds)
  • 1 tablespoon of Banga spice leaves (Beletete) and dried bitter leaves

Instructions

Step 1: How to extract the banga juice from fresh banga nuts

  1. Boil the palm kernel fruit for 30 minutes until it becomes soft
  2. Transfer the nuts into a mortar and pound gently, making sure you don't break the nuts
  3. Pour some hot water over the pounded nuts, squeeze and use a sieve to strain out the extract - do this a couple of times until you get all the extract from the nut.
  4. Pour the extract into a pot and boil until it thickens and the oil rises to the top.

Step 2: How to wash your fresh cat fish

  1. Cut your fish into pieces, seperating the head, mid sections and tail
  2. Pour hot (but not boiling) water over the fish to congeal the slime, making it easier to remove.
  3. Use a knife to scrape off all sliminess on the fish skin
  4. Rinse with cold water to wash away the slime, repeating the process if necessary.

Step 3: How to prepare the banga soup

  1. Rinse the meat, add the diced onions, Cameroon pepper, seasoning powder, and salt. Stir and leave to cook till the meat becomes tender.
  2. Add the stockfish and cook for another 10 minutes.
  3. Remove from the meat, stockfish, and meat sock from the pot and set aside.
  4. Pour the palm nut concentrate inside the same pot. Dilute with some water and leftover meat stock from the boiled meat. Cover and leave to cook until the oil begins to float on top of the soup.
  5. Meanwhile, blend the scotch bonnet pepper and the remaining half onion and add to the palm nut.
  6. Add the Banga spice, smoked fish, meat, seasoning powder, salt, crayfish, and Banga stick. Cover and leave to cook for another 10 minutes.
  7. Stir and add in the beletete and bitter leaves. Leave to simmer till thickened to your taste.
  8. Serve hot with starch, pounded yam, semolina, garri or fufu

Tips for preparing delicious banga soup

  1. Season and spice to your level:Banga soup requires various spice which adds flavor and taste to your soup, so ensure to taste as you go to avoid over spicing your soup.
  2. Good finishing: Ensure to finish off your soup by adding beletete, as this leaves helps to enhance the flavor and taste of your soup.
  3. Thicken your soup: To enjoy your soup, allow it to get thick with the oil floating on top before turning off the heat.
